Why Can't Hashes Be Reversed?

A cryptographic hash function is a one-way mathematical operation. Given a hash output, it is computationally infeasible to recover the original input without iterating through every possible input.

bcrypt goes further — it uses a unique random salt per password and applies 2cost iterations of Blowfish cipher, making brute-force attacks extremely slow even with modern hardware.
